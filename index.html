About

A bit about me

Not the usual "passionate engineer" spiel — just what I actually do and why it matters.

I've spent the last 3+ years writing performance-critical software — mostly in the world of warehouse robotics, where I built the systems that let autonomous mobile robots navigate, detect obstacles, and dock themselves with millimeter precision.

My core skillset is modern C++ (17/20), GPU computing, and real-time systems on Linux/POSIX. Day-to-day that means writing multithreaded perception pipelines, optimizing YOLO models through TensorRT for 30+ FPS on embedded GPUs, and caring deeply about latency, memory, and thread safety.

The part I enjoy most? Taking something from "kind of works on a good day" to "runs 12 hours straight without intervention." That applies whether it's an autonomous robot, an ML inference service, or a spatial computing pipeline. I obsess over profiling, tuning, and making systems bulletproof.

I'm now looking to take these skills into general AI/ML engineering, autonomous vehicles, SLAM/spatial computing, or any role that involves writing fast C++, shipping real-time inference, or building systems where performance matters. The domain is less important than the craft.
